Examination Rules & Guidelines

These examination rules are prescribed to ensure fairness, transparency, and discipline during undergraduate and postgraduate examinations. Every learner is expected to strictly adhere to these guidelines.

üÜî Identity & Entry Regulations

Every learner must carry a valid college identity card and hall ticket to the examination hall. Invigilators are authorized to verify the identity of learners at any time during the examination.

Entry into the examination hall without proper identification or after the scheduled reporting time may result in denial of entry or disciplinary action.

üìù Examination Pattern & Duration

Examinations are conducted as per the approved scheme, consisting of Internal Continuous Assessment and Semester End Examination. The duration of the semester-end examination is generally two and a half hours.

Learners must strictly follow the question paper pattern prescribed by the respective Board of Studies.

üìä Passing Standards

To pass a course, a learner must secure a minimum of forty percent marks in aggregate as well as separately in the Internal Assessment and Semester End Examination components, wherever applicable.

Failure to meet the minimum passing criteria in any component results in the learner being declared unsuccessful in that course.

üîÅ Re-Examination Rules

Learners who do not satisfy the passing standards are permitted to appear for re-examinations conducted after the declaration of results. Re-examinations are held once in an academic year.

Submission of an online re-examination application along with payment of prescribed fees within the stipulated deadline is mandatory.

üìö Conduct Inside the Examination Hall

Learners must maintain complete silence and discipline inside the examination hall. Any form of communication with other learners, use of unauthorized materials, or deviation from instructions issued by invigilators is strictly prohibited.

All instructions given by the examination authorities must be followed without argument or delay.

üìÑ Answer Book Guidelines

Learners must write answers only in the provided answer books and supplementary sheets. Writing names, roll numbers, symbols, or any identifying marks inside the answer book is not permitted.

Any insertion, removal, or tampering with answer books is treated as a serious breach of examination rules.

üìà Grading & Evaluation System

The college follows a ten-point grading system for declaration of results, which includes calculation of Grade Point Average (GPA) and Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA).

Final results are declared only after successful completion of all examinations within the prescribed validity period of the program.

‚ö†Ô∏è General Compliance

Any act not explicitly mentioned in the rules but considered as misconduct by the examination authorities will be treated as a violation of examination regulations.

The college reserves the right to modify examination rules from time to time, and such modifications are binding on all learners.

These examination rules are framed in accordance with the official Examination Guidelines for Undergraduate and Postgraduate Programs.
