Smuggling answer books, inserting additional sheets, or writing answers outside the examination hall constitutes tampering.

Forging invigilator signatures, altering official seals, or manipulating examination documents is considered an act of fraud.

Appearing for an examination on behalf of another learner, or allowing someone else to appear, is classified as impersonation.

Attempting to bribe examination staff, use abusive language, or threaten invigilators and authorities is a serious breach of discipline.

Learners committing repeated offences or engaging in severe forms of malpractice are liable for enhanced punishment.
